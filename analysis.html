MEAM 6900 · Spring 2026

Rocket Combustion Chamber CFD

GOX/GCH₄ combustion and supersonic nozzle flow simulated in ANSYS Fluent with a species-transport combustion model. Independent parametric sweeps of back pressure, O/F mixture ratio, and injector swirl: rising back pressure suppressed nozzle choking and introduced normal shocks, while mixture-ratio sweeps from fuel-rich to ox-rich shifted flame temperature with peak values near MR ≈ 4 and left the flow structure largely unchanged. With Oren Minsk and Eitan Seitchik.

MEAM 5360 · Spring 2026

Flow Past a Circular Cylinder

2D incompressible flow past a confined cylinder at Re = 0.1–1000 in ANSYS Fluent. A mesh-independence study settled on an 11k-element hybrid mesh with boundary-layer inflation; drag coefficients were benchmarked against empirical correlations, and transient runs captured the transition from creeping flow to periodic Kármán vortex shedding at Re ≥ 100. With Jason de Gentile.

ENM 5220 · Spring 2026

ADI Solver for Turing Patterns

A Peaceman–Rachford ADI solver for the Schnakenberg reaction-diffusion system on the unit square, written from scratch in MATLAB: hand-derived discretization, careful Neumann boundary treatment at each half-step, and Thomas-algorithm tridiagonal solves. Reproduces the diffusion-driven pattern formation results of Madzvamuse & Maini (2007) at γ = 114, 1000, and 5000, with a convergence study against a known diffusion solution.

MEAM 3480 · 2025

Maglev Train

A magnetically levitated train with linear synchronous motor propulsion: N52 permanent-magnet levitation, air-core electromagnet coils driven by H-bridge controllers with Hall-effect commutation, and a two-part modular carriage with adjustable ballast to tune levitation height and lateral stability.

MEAM 2301 · Spring 2025

Recumbent Bicycle Windshield

CFD study of windshield geometries for a recumbent bicycle, targeting rider comfort rather than pure drag reduction. Velocity-field analysis around the rider at low and high speeds compared flat, interpolated, and curved shield profiles, focusing on flow conditions at the rider's head. With Annie Gao and Ari Weiner.
